Analysis¶

Shift $X$ to $\bar{X}$ by substracing the mean of each column $$\bar{X} = U\Sigma V^{T}$$ where where $\Sigma$ is a diagonal matrix containing the singular values $\sigma_{i}$ of each principle component

The i-th explain variance is $$\lambda_{i} = \frac{\sigma_{i}^{2}}{n-1}$$ where n is the number of samples in the data

Result Interpretation¶

  • Factor loadings represent the correlations between the observed variables (y-axis) and the latent factors (x-axis)
  • High Loadings, observed variables and factors have strong association (typically >0.4 or < -0.4)
  • Sign of Loadings, the sign (positive or negative) indicates the direction of the relationship

Eigenvalues¶

  • Eigenvalue > 1 Rule, retain factor_1 and factor_2 as they have eigenvalues greater than 1 or near 1
  • Scree Plot, look for the elbow point, which supports retaining two factors

Interpretation¶

  • Interpreting Factors, a factor can be interpreted by looking at which observed variables have high loadings on it
  • sepal length, petal length, and petal width have high loadings with factor_1, which may represent flower volumn
  • sepal width have high loading with factor_2, which may represent sepal volumn

Communalities¶

  • Indicate the proportion of each observed variable’s variance that can be explained by the factors

  • $0.52^{2} + 0.38^{2} = 0.41$

  • High Communalities, sepal width has communality above 0.925, suggesting it is well-represented by the two factors

  • Low Communality, sepal length, petal length, and petal width has low comunalities, suggesting they are not well-represented by the two factors
